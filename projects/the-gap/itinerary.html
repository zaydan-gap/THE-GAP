Program Overview

90 Days.
Three Phases.

This is not a gap year. It is not a vacation. It is not a program.
It is the thing that should have happened before you were handed a life you didn't choose.

Every culture that understood something about human development had a rite of passage — a moment where a young person was sent into difficulty and returned as themselves. We don't have that anymore. We have resume builders and Instagram trips. THE GAP is what should have existed.

The Three Phases
Phase One · NZ
Decondition

The world has been building a version of you for twenty years. Physical challenge, cold water, distance, and silence dismantle it — not with therapy, but with presence. You cannot maintain a false self when your nervous system is fully occupied being alive.

Phase Two · SE Asia
Encounter

You will meet people who chose themselves. Masters. Warriors. Watermen. People who look nothing like what society rewards and everything like what a real life looks like. You learn not by taking notes but by moving alongside them.

Phase Three · LATAM
Return

Everything you found gets given back. You don't return as the same person — you return as the real one. And the real one leads. The real one shows others the door. This is where the program becomes something larger than itself.

Daily Standards

The Non-Negotiables

These happen every single day of 90 days. No exceptions. No rest days. Miss them and you're just traveling.

01 — Every Morning
Cold Exposure

Ocean, river, cold shower — before anything else. Not about health. About choosing to do something hard before the world has asked anything of you. It rewires what you think is possible before breakfast.

02 — Every Morning
Morning Pages

3 pages, handwritten, before touching your phone. Not a journal — a brain dump. Let every thought out. Most people have never met their own mind. This is how.

03 — Every Week
The Week's Question

One question per week. Don't answer it on Day 1 — live inside it. Carry it through every experience. By Day 7 it will have answered itself in ways you couldn't have predicted.

04 — Every Day
4 Hours Phone-Free

Awake, present, unreachable. The phone is the primary mechanism through which the false self maintains itself. Remove the feed and see what's left.

05 — Every Day
One Real Conversation

Not "where are you from." Ask something that matters. "What's the best decision you ever made?" "What do you know now you wish you'd known at 20?" The world is full of people with answers you haven't thought to seek.

06 — Every Evening
Evening Write

20–30 minutes responding to the week's question. Don't try to answer it — write what the day stirred up. Over 90 days, this becomes the most honest document you will ever create about yourself.

Daily Structure

This schedule adapts to every location and activity — but the structure holds everywhere. The rhythm is what makes you feel like yourself when you get home.

DAY 01
Auckland → Remote South Island Bach
Arrival
Arrival — Into the Quiet
Morning / Transit
Land Auckland. No city. Pick up rental car, drive south immediately to a remote bach — a NZ cabin, no neighbors, no wifi. This is your first 24 hours. Arrive before dark.
Afternoon
Unpack slowly. Walk the property. Find water — river, beach, or stream. Go in. First cold exposure. Stay in until your mind stops arguing with your body. That gap between mind and body is the entire territory of this program.
Evening
One hour writing: not about the trip — about who you were the day before you left. Write it like a character study. Get specific: how did you talk, what did you perform, what were you afraid people would find out?
The Purpose
You can't find yourself by jumping straight into stimulation. The first act is to put distance — real distance — between you and everything that was telling you who to be.

First Silence — Meeting Your Own Mind
Morning
Cold exposure. Morning pages. Then 2-hour walk with no destination, no headphones. When you feel the urge to check your phone, notice it and don't. That urge is information. What are you trying to avoid?
Afternoon
Sit outside for one hour doing nothing. Not meditating — just sitting. Notice what your mind does when you don't give it a task. Most people have never done this. It will be uncomfortable. It is supposed to be.
Evening Write

DAY 04
Routeburn Track — Overnight
Overnight
Overnight on the Track — Who Are You in the Dark?
Full Day
Pack for overnight. Walk 8–10 hours solo on the Routeburn Track. Camp overnight. No phone signal. The southern sky is visible here in a way that physically stops you.
The Principle
When your body is working hard to cover ground, the mind doesn't have bandwidth for performance. Notice who you are when that is all you are — just a body moving through landscape.
Evening Write (by headlamp)
"What did I stop performing today? What was underneath it?"
The Purpose
The overnight is the first real test. You cannot maintain an image in a tent in the dark. Whatever shows up alone on a mountain at night is the real material.

DAY 09
Waipoua Forest, Northland
Encounter
Waipoua Forest — Whakapapa
Full Day
Spend the day with a Maori guide in Waipoua Forest — home to kauri trees 2,000 years old. Your guide teaches you about whakapapa: you are not just yourself. You are all the people who came before you. You carry your ancestors in your body. You exist in a line, not in isolation.
The Tension
The western concept: you are an individual. Whakapapa: you are a continuation. Both are true. Before you can find yourself, you need to understand what "self" actually means. The Maori answer is radically different from what school taught you.
Evening Write
"Who came before me that I carry without knowing? What did they give me? What did they put in me that I didn't choose?"

Week 3
Queenstown — Adrenaline as Truth Serum
"When does the performance disappear?"

Adrenaline is not entertainment. It is a neurological reset. In the three seconds before you jump, your mind cannot maintain a story about who you are. The social masks dissolve. Only the real you exists there. This week uses adrenaline as an instrument for truth.

Evening

DAY 16
Kawarau Bridge
Adrenaline
THE BUNGEE — AJ Hackett Kawarau Bridge (43m)
The Experience
The original bungee. 43 meters. Before the platform, notice your breathing. On the platform, notice what your brain says — it will offer thousands of reasons not to jump. Notice none of them are real. The moment you stop arguing and just go is one of the most honest experiences available to a human being.
Immediate Protocol
After landing — before talking to anyone — find somewhere quiet and write for 20 minutes. Every sensation. Every thought. What happened in the 3 seconds before you jumped? Who were you in that moment?
Evening Write
"What parts of my daily life am I hesitating at the edge of? What is the equivalent of the platform? What is the equivalent of just going?"
The Purpose
You cannot perform in that moment. Only the real you exists at the edge. The write immediately after is the entire point — capture who that person was before the rational mind reclaims the experience.
DAY 17
Shotover River
Adrenaline
Shotover River Whitewater — Who Are You Under Pressure?
Full Day
Class IV whitewater with strangers. This is different from the bungee — it's a team experience. You and a river that does not care about your feelings. Notice how you move with others under real pressure. Do you lead? Freeze? Become more yourself or less?
Observation
No judgment — only observation. The ring shows you what the classroom hides. Your behavior under low-stakes pressure is a preview of who you are under high-stakes pressure.
Evening Write
"Who am I in a team under pressure? What did I do that surprised me? What did I wish I'd done differently?"
DAY 18
15,000 ft, Queenstown
Adrenaline
THE SKYDIVE — 60 Seconds of Complete Presence
The Experience
15,000 feet, tandem. The plane ride is 20 minutes of sitting with pure anticipatory fear. Don't manage it. Don't perform calm. Let it be there. Freefall: 60 seconds at 200km/h. In freefall, there is no past and no future. Only the present moment. Most people spend their entire lives slightly behind or ahead of now. This is fully here.
Immediate Protocol
After landing: 3 pages immediately. What happened to you up there? What was freefall like in your body? And — the most important question — when else in your life do you feel that present? If the answer is "never," that is the most important thing this program will ever show you.
Evening Write
"What would my life look like if I lived at the level of presence I felt in freefall?"

DAYS 30–32
Amed, East Bali
Freediving
AIDA Freediving Course — The Quietest Place
Day 30 — Theory + Pool
Begin AIDA Level 2 freediving. Theory: how breathing actually works, full diaphragmatic breath, breath-hold physiology. Your lungs have twice the capacity you normally use. You will learn that the ocean rewards stillness. So does life. School never taught you how to cultivate it.
Day 31 — First Real Dives
Open water, 10 meters on a single breath. Under the surface: no sound except your own heartbeat. No past. No future. Just pressure, water, and faint blue light above you. The quietest place you will ever go. Some people cry after the first real freedive — not from fear, from relief.
Day 32 — Certification
AIDA cert dives — 16-meter target depth. You are doing something today on a single breath that your mind said was impossible when you started. Every time the mind says "I can't," this dive is the evidence that the mind is a liar.
The Purpose
Freediving teaches that stillness is a skill. The body's panic reflex is a story the mind tells, not a physical reality. What else in your life has your mind labeled "impossible" that your body could handle if you let it?
DAYS 33–34

Homestay
Balinese Family Homestay — Daily Offering
The Setup
This is not a hotel. You eat with the family, help cook, sit in the compound in the evenings. The Balinese make offerings every single morning — small baskets of flowers and incense at every doorway, altar, corner. Before they do anything for themselves, they acknowledge the world.
Day 34

Weeks 7–8

You cannot grow in the presence of comfort. In the ring — even sparring lightly — every social mask you wear falls away. You cannot perform toughness when someone can actually hit you. What remains is real. This is not about becoming a fighter. It is about two weeks in the presence of something that requires your complete and total honesty.

Your body revolts. Everything hurts. Trainers show you no mercy — not because they don't care, because they see what you can do and refuse to settle for less. Train anyway.
Days 4–6: The Wall
Almost everyone wants to quit. Novelty has worn off. Soreness has peaked. Skill isn't there yet. This is the exact moment the program is built around. Choosing to stay is when you begin to meet yourself.

Day 7 — something shifts. You stop thinking and start moving. The trainer nods once. Day 10: write specifically — "Who was I on Day 1? Who am I now? What can I do with my body that I couldn't ten days ago?"
Sparring Protocol
The first time another person's glove touches your face, the part of your brain that manages your public image goes offline. You stop thinking about how you look. You respond. Who you are in that response — without the filter — is the most honest data point this program offers.

Day Five
Solo

Each participant spends 4 hours alone in nature. No phone. No agenda. Just being. Some will love it. Some will panic. Both are information. Evening circle: "What came up in the silence? What does your own company feel like?" This is always the most powerful circle of the week.

Day 89
Day Six
Create

Each person creates something — a letter, a drawing, a manifesto, a movement piece, anything. It matters that it's real. That it reflects who they actually are, not who they perform being. Afternoon: each person shares what they made. You witness. You don't evaluate. There is a difference between those two things.

The Closing

Final cold plunge as a group. Final morning pages. Closing circle — each person answers three questions, you answer last: "Who did you arrive as? Who are you leaving as? What are you doing differently?" After the last person speaks, hold one full minute of silence. Let everything that happened in seven days be real. Then close it. The world school has begun.
