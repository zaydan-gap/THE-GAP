The Program

Wild every day.
Together the whole way.

This is not a retreat. It is not a therapy program. It is not structured personal development dressed up as adventure.

It is 90 days of doing the hardest, most alive things imaginable — in places that strip away everything ordinary — with a group of people chasing the same feeling. The feeling that you are actually living. The feeling most people get a glimpse of and then spend the rest of their lives trying to find again.

THE GAP puts you in that feeling every single day. And then shows you how to carry it home.

Three Phases
Phase 1 · NZ
Decondition

Get as far from ordinary life as the earth allows. Replace comfort with cold water, hard terrain, and the kind of adrenaline that makes every social mask drop. You learn who you actually are when survival is on the line.

Phase 2 · SE Asia
Encounter

Meet masters. Go underwater on one breath. Fight in a ring. Train with people who have built their entire lives around one discipline. You absorb what commitment looks like from the inside.

Phase 3 · LATAM
Return

Take everything you found and give it back. Serve communities. Lead the group through a week-long prototype. Turn what you lived into something that others can step into.

Group Dynamics

This program runs with 10–20 people. The group is not just company — it is part of the program. You will push each other, fail in front of each other, laugh hard, move through genuinely hard things together, and build friendships that come from shared experience at depth. The group makes the individual. The individual makes the group stronger.

01 — Every Morning
Cold Exposure Together

Ocean, river, or cold shower — every morning as a group before anything else. Competitive, loud, chaotic. The one non-negotiable that turns strangers into a crew within a week.

02 — Every Day
One Wild Thing

Every single day has at least one experience that is genuinely wild — something most people would never do, something that requires your full presence. No filler days.

03 — Weekly
Solo Time

Once a week, every person gets a significant block of time completely alone in nature. This is the most important part of the program for most people — and the part most people are most afraid of.

04 — Every Evening
Come Together

Group dinner every night. Campfires when possible. No phones at the table. Stories come out here that wouldn't come out anywhere else. No format — just real people eating together.

05 — Personal
Reflect Your Way

Journaling, talking, running, sitting — whatever works for you. There is no mandatory reflection format. You process how you process. What matters is that you don't just move through these experiences without letting them land.

06 — Always
Show Up for the Group

You are responsible for your own experience and accountable to the group. If someone is struggling, you notice. If someone needs pushing, you push. The group takes care of the group.

DAY 01
Auckland → South Island
Group
Arrival — The Group Meets
Morning
Group lands in Auckland. No sightseeing, no airport hotel. Fleet of vans drives south immediately. You are going somewhere remote.
Afternoon
Arrive at a remote coastal property — a camp, not a resort. Gear unpacked, rooms sorted. First cold exposure as a group: everyone in the sea before dinner. Competitive, chaotic, freezing. The first shared thing.
Evening
Group dinner on the property. Campfire. Opening circle: everyone says one sentence about why they're here. No speeches. No performance. One sentence.
DAY 02
South Island Coast
Adventure
Dawn Run + Cliff Jump + Open Water Swim
Dawn
5:30 AM group cold ocean plunge. Then a 5km coastal run together — no one gets left behind. The first time you run with 15 strangers toward a cold ocean will feel like something.
Morning
Cliff jumping — find the local spots. Start small, go bigger. Group watches, cheers, goes together. The person who goes first every time is learning something about themselves. So is the person who goes last.
Afternoon
Open water group swim — 1km. No stopping. Stick together. This is not a race. It is a group moving through water as one thing.
DAY 03
South Island Trails
Adventure
Trail Run + Overnight Pack-In
Morning
Cold ocean. Pack for overnight. Each person carries their own gear plus shares on group equipment. The logistics teach something: who steps up, who waits to be told, who thinks ahead.
Full Day
Trail run / hike into a backcountry campsite — 15–20km. Some will lead, some will suffer. The group moves at the group's pace. If someone falls back, two people fall back with them.
Evening
Camp together in the wilderness. No wifi. Cook on fire. Sleep under the southern sky — the Milky Way here is something most of the group has never seen. Sleep on the ground. This is the first real night.
DAY 04
Backcountry Camp
Group
Wilderness Day — No Agenda
Morning
Cold stream. Group breakfast cooked together over fire. Slow morning in the wilderness — no rush, no schedule. Let the land have you for a few hours.
Afternoon
Split into pairs: each pair spends 3 hours exploring, climbing, swimming — wherever feels right. No phones. No goal. Just two people in wild country figuring out how to move together.
Evening
Pack out. Return to base. Campfire night — stories from the pairs. What did you find?
DAY 05
South Island
Solo
First Solo — 4 Hours Alone
Morning
Cold ocean together, then the group disperses. Every person finds their own spot — at least 500 meters from anyone else. No phone. No book. No agenda. 4 hours alone in New Zealand wilderness.
The Experience
Some people will sleep. Some will explore. Some will sit. Some will cry and not know why. All of it is fine. The solo hour shows you things about yourself that no amount of activity ever reaches.
Afternoon
Regroup. Group debrief — whoever wants to share, shares. No pressure. What came up in the silence? What surprised you? This conversation is always one of the best ones of the whole program.
Why Solo Time
You cannot find yourself in a group. The group gives you energy, momentum, belonging. The solo gives you truth. Both are necessary. The program alternates between them deliberately.
DAY 06
Coastal Town
Adventure
Learn to Surf — Group Challenge
Full Day
Group surf lesson. Beginner waves. Everyone starts at zero. The group suddenly has something in common: falling down and laughing about it. Competition emerges naturally — who can stand up first, who can ride longest. Let it.
The Insight
Falling in water has zero ego attached. You fall, you laugh, you paddle back. Notice how different this feels from failing at something at home where everyone watches and remembers. What if normal life worked like this?
Evening
Fish and chips on the beach. Group dinner in the sand. Stories about who ate it hardest. This is what the evenings become.
DAY 07
South Island
Rest + Play
Free Day — Explore, Recover, Connect
Morning
Cold ocean as always. Then: totally unstructured morning. Sleep in. Read. Move. Explore. Do whatever you want. The absence of agenda is its own kind of wild.
Afternoon
Group organizes their own activity — vote and go. Whatever the group decides. First time you collectively choose something. Notice how the group moves when there's no leader directing.
Evening
Group dinner, full house. Week 1 check-in: go around the table — one word for how Week 1 has been. No explanation required.

DAY 08
South Island → Northland
Transit + Wild
Drive North — Swim Every River
Full Day
Road trip north in the vans. Group rule for the drive: every time you cross a river or see a waterfall, you stop and everyone swims. No exceptions. This turns a transit day into an adventure. You'll stop 4–8 times.
The Rule
If the van stops, everyone goes in. No watching from the bank. This is the day the group develops its first real culture — the one that says: when we stop, we go.
Evening

DAY 09
Waipoua Forest
Key Day
Waipoua Forest — Maori Guide, Ancient Trees, Whakapapa
Full Day
Full day with a Maori guide in Waipoua Forest. The kauri trees here are 2,000 years old. Your guide teaches the group about whakapapa — the concept that you are not just yourself, you are all the people who came before you. You carry your ancestors in your body. You exist in a line, not alone.
The Encounter
This is the first real encounter of the program — a person who carries a completely different understanding of selfhood. The western self is individual. The Maori self is relational. Both are true. The group sits with a 2,000-year-old tree and feels the weight of that.
Evening
Campfire. Open conversation: what did the forest show you? No facilitator. Just the group talking in the dark.
Why This Day Exists
Before you can find yourself, you need to understand what "self" actually means. The Maori answer is radically different from what school taught the group. This day plants a question that grows for the rest of 90 days.
DAY 10
Bay of Islands
Water
Group Ocean Kayak + Snorkel + Night Swim
Morning
4-hour group kayak through the Bay of Islands — 144 islands to paddle between. No motorized support. If someone falls out, the group stops and helps them back in. Racing is allowed and encouraged.
Afternoon
Snorkel the reef — 1 hour underwater as a group. Buddy system. Explore. Find the wild things. The ocean rewards going deeper.
Night
Night swim in bioluminescent water if conditions allow — the bay has it. Swimming through glowing blue water at night with 15 people is an experience that belongs to no category.
DAY 11
Northland
Service
Maori Youth Program — Lead a Morning
Morning
The group runs a morning session with a local Maori youth outdoor program. The group designs and leads it — movement, games, a trail, swimming. No one tells you how. Figure it out together. Lead something together for the first time.
Afternoon
Play. Get competitive with the local kids. Let them beat you. This is not charity — it is two groups of young people spending a day together. The difference is invisible when you're actually in it.
Evening
Group debrief: what does it feel like to give something without keeping score? What came up?
Service Rule
Every service moment in this program follows the same principle: show up, move alongside people, leave them feeling more capable than when you arrived. Not charity. Not saving. Just showing up fully and being human together.

Full group surf session — building on Day 6. Everyone is getting better. Natural competition: who progresses fastest? Who helps others? Who's coaching from the beach vs. in the water? Character reveals itself in optional moments.
Day 13
Group surf competition — informal, self-organized. Points for style, for commitment, for the worst wipeout, for helping someone stand up. The awards are made up. The joy is real.

Week 3
Queenstown — Truth Serum
Adrenaline as an instrument

In the three seconds before you jump, your mind cannot maintain a story about who you are. The performance drops. The social mask falls. Only the real you exists at the edge. The group watches each other be real — and nothing builds trust faster.

DAY 16
Kawarau Bridge
Milestone
THE BUNGEE — Group Watches Each Other Jump
The Setup
AJ Hackett Kawarau Bridge. The original bungee. 43 meters. The whole group goes — one by one, while everyone else watches from the bridge and banks below. No one skips. No one is excluded.
The Group Element
Watching your friends at the edge — the fear, the hesitation, the moment they go — is as powerful as doing it yourself. You see who they actually are in that three-second window. The group sees each other differently after this.
After
Group lunch together. The energy is high, the conversation is honest. What came up for you? What did you see in the person next to you?
DAY 17
Shotover River
Adrenaline
Shotover Whitewater — Group Rafts
Full Day
Shotover River Class IV whitewater in group rafts. Split into crews of 6–8. The crew that works together most effectively survives the cleanest. You'll find out who leads under pressure, who panics, who becomes strangely calm when everything is chaotic.
Competition
Race format between boats where the river allows. Bragging rights only. The energy in the boat when you're working together against the water is one of the best group experiences available.
Evening
Group dinner. Tell the story of the day. Reenactments encouraged.
DAY 18
15,000 ft, Queenstown
Milestone
THE SKYDIVE — 15,000 Feet Together
Morning
The whole group skydives. 15,000 feet, tandem. The plane ride up is 20 minutes with 15 people in varying states of controlled terror. The energy in that plane is unlike anything. Then the door opens.
Freefall
60 seconds at 200km/h. In freefall there is no past and no future. There is only now. Most people have never been fully present for 60 consecutive seconds. This is what fully alive feels like at maximum intensity.
After
The group reconvenes on the ground. The conversation that happens naturally in the hour after everyone has jumped is one of the most honest conversations the group will ever have. Let it run.
DAY 19
Queenstown
Adrenaline
Canyon Swing + Via Ferrata + Luge
Morning
Canyon Swing — 60-meter free-fall, group watches each other. Same dynamic as the bungee but different because you choose your jump position. The choice is part of it.
Afternoon
Via ferrata climbing in the Remarkables — technical movement on real rock. Group belays each other. Physical problem-solving at altitude. Your body knows more than you think.
Evening
Queenstown Skyline Luge — fast, stupid fun. Sometimes the program needs to just be fun. Let it be. Race each other. The person who wins gloats appropriately.
DAY 20
The Remarkables
Mountain
Summit Together — Earn the View
Full Day
Group hike to a summit in the Remarkables — 4–5 hours up. No one goes faster than the slowest person. The group moves as one. At the top: everyone arrives together, sits for 30 minutes, no phones, just the view and each other.
The Rule
The view belongs to everyone who earned it. You don't get the summit photo if you left people behind to get it. This is a principle the group needs to internalize.
Evening
Big group dinner in Queenstown. Week 3 has been the most intense. Let the evening be long and relaxed.
DAY 21
Queenstown Region
Solo
Solo Day — Queenstown on Your Own
Full Day
Everyone disperses alone for the full day. No group plan. Go wherever feels right — the town, the mountains, the lake, a café, nothing. The group has been intense. The solo day lets the individual breathe inside the experience.
Options
Paraglide alone, kayak the lake, find a trail no one else knows about, sit by the water for four hours, talk to locals, explore, rest. Your call entirely.
Evening
Everyone reconvenes for dinner. The solo stories are always different and always good.

DAY 22

Fox Glacier + Ice Hike
Full Day
Group hike on Fox Glacier — crampons on, ice underfoot, blue crevasses, the sound of a 10,000-year-old thing moving. These glaciers are disappearing faster than almost anywhere on earth. Walk on something irreplaceable.

DAY 23

Every River — Group Road Trip Rule
Full Day
Group rule: every river the vans cross on the West Coast highway, everyone swims. The west coast has rivers of impossible blue — glacial runoff, cold enough to stop your breath. Stop at every one. Everyone in. No exceptions.
The Day
This is one of the most joyful days of the whole program. No agenda. No goals. Just a van full of people swimming in every body of water they encounter. The West Coast will deliver 6–10 stops.

DAY 24
Milford Sound
Milestone
Milford Sound — Group Kayak Through Waterfalls
Full Day
Group kayak through Milford Sound — one of the most beautiful places on earth. Waterfalls come down directly into the kayaks. Seals. Dolphins if lucky. Fiords so deep the water is black. Paddle 6 hours together.
The Moment
At some point during the paddle, the group will all stop, floating on the water in silence, surrounded by a fiord. No one will say anything. That silence — shared — is one of the group's defining memories.

DAYS 30–32
Amed, East Bali
Freediving
AIDA Freediving Course — Group Goes Underwater
Day 30 — Theory
Group AIDA Level 1/2 freediving course begins. Theory: how breathing actually works, breath-hold physics, equalizing. Your lungs have twice the capacity you normally use. The ocean rewards stillness. School never taught you how to cultivate it.
Day 31 — Open Water
First real dives — 10 meters on a single breath. Under the surface: your own heartbeat and nothing else. No past, no future. The quietest place the group has ever been. Some people cry after their first real freedive. Both are fine. The group holds space for each other.
Day 32 — Depth
AIDA certification dives — 15–20m target depth. The group is doing something on a single breath that the mind said was impossible three days ago. Remember this. The mind is a liar about limits.
The Group Freediving Experience
There is something profound about a group of people going underwater together in silence. The debrief after certification dives is always one of the most honest conversations of the whole program. Something about the depth brings things to the surface.
DAYS 33–34
Amed
Water
Reef Cleanup + Cliff Jumping + Night Dive
Day 33
Group reef cleanup dive with local dive shops. The group was given something extraordinary by this ocean. Give something back. Two hours underwater removing what doesn't belong. Then: cliff jumping at Blue Lagoon — pure joy after the seriousness of the cleanup.
Day 34
Night dive in Amed — one of the best night dive spots in Asia. The reef transforms after dark. Bioluminescence, sleeping fish, octopus. The group goes underwater at night together. This sounds terrifying to most people. It is also extraordinary.
Evening
Group dinner in Amed village. Eat what the locals eat. Eat together.
DAY 35
Ubud
Breathwork
Group Wim Hof Breathwork — Internal Power
Morning
Group breathwork session with a practitioner — Wim Hof method. The group breathes together in a way that produces physiological states none of you have experienced before. Strange things happen in a breathwork session. Even stranger things happen when you do it in a room of 15 people simultaneously.
The Revelation
You can change how you feel from the inside. This is the lesson underneath the technique. You have been waiting for the outside world — the right circumstances, relationship, achievement — to make you feel a certain way. This session shows the group that the power was always internal.
Afternoon
Free afternoon in Ubud. Explore, eat, move, recover. Let the morning integrate. The town is full of interesting things and interesting people.
DAY 36
Uluwatu
Sacred
Uluwatu — Pre-Dawn Surf, 1,000-Year-Old Temple
Pre-Dawn
Group drives to Uluwatu at 5 AM. Cold ocean. Watch the sunrise from the cliff. A 1,000-year-old Hindu temple sits above one of the most consistent surf breaks in the world.
Dawn Surf
Group surfs Uluwatu at first light. The temple is above you. The Indian Ocean is beneath you. Sacred and physical are the same thing here. This is Bali's lesson at full volume.
Evening
Kecak fire dance at Uluwatu temple at sunset — 100 male voices, no instruments, just human bodies making something enormous. Group watches together. Let it land.
DAYS 37–38
Bali
Volcano + Village
Volcano Sunrise Hike + Village Day
Day 37 — Mt. Batur
Night hike up Mt. Batur — an active volcano. 2 AM start, headlamps, dark trail, summit at sunrise. The crater edge with the whole group at sunrise over a volcanic caldera lake. Eat breakfast on the rim. This is one of the group's top three memories.
Day 38 — Munduk
Munduk mountain village — rice terraces, waterfalls, mist. The group spends the day with a local family: help with the morning, walk the terraces, eat together. Find an elder and ask: "What do you know now that you wish you'd known at our age?"
Evening
Return to base. Easy evening. The group has been going hard. Slow dinner, early sleep.

Weeks 7–8
Muay Thai Camp — Fire
Train like fighters. Become something different.

This is not about becoming a fighter. It is about two weeks in the presence of something that requires your complete and total honesty. In the ring, every social mask falls away. What remains is real. The group sees each other without the masks. That changes everything.

Days 1–3: The Shock
The group's body revolts. Everything hurts. Trainers show no mercy — because they see what the group can do. Day 3 morning: half the group questions everything. Train anyway. This is what the program is built on.
Days 4–6: The Wall
Almost everyone wants to quit. This is the exact moment. The group helps each other through it — showing up to the 6 AM run for the person next to you when you wouldn't for yourself. The group IS the program at this point.
Days 7–14: Inside It
Day 7 — something shifts. The group stops fighting the training and starts moving inside it. Sparring begins in earnest. The group fights each other. The group fights strangers. The group sees each other without any masks at all.
The Sparring Reality
When the group sparrs each other — when your friend's glove touches your face — something changes. You see each other as you actually are, not as you present. Nothing in this program builds trust inside the group faster than shared physical honesty. The friendships that come out of these 14 days are the ones that last.

DAY 53
Muay Thai Camp
Service

Afternoon
Most camps have local kids training. The group spends the afternoon holding pads, teaching footwork, working with the youngest fighters. The group learned this 12 days ago. Already they can teach someone else. Notice what that means.

DAY 55
Muay Thai Camp
Fight Night
GROUP FIGHT NIGHT — Walk Into the Ring
The Event
The group organizes a fight night — white collar bouts, anyone who wants to fight fights. The whole group watches. Trainers referee. Local fighters invited. This is a celebration of what the group built in two weeks, not a test of who's best.
The Walk
The walk to the ring — 30 meters — is the entire camp compressed into a moment. Every story your mind tells about who you are gets tested. The group cheers every person who walks in. That includes the ones who are terrified. Especially the ones who are terrified.
After
Camp celebration. Everyone. The group is something completely different than it was 14 days ago. Acknowledge that.

DAY 58
Medellín
Arrival
Medellín — The City That Rebuilt Itself
Morning
First thing: cable car to the comunas — the hillside neighborhoods that were the epicenter of the violence 30 years ago. Stand there. Look at the city below. Read about what happened here and what they chose to build instead. Let the story land before you do anything else.
Afternoon
Walk the real city. El Centro, the Metrocable barrios, the Botero Plaza. Talk to people. Ask about their city. Colombians talk about their city with a specific kind of pride that comes from having rebuilt it themselves.
Evening
Group dinner in a local restaurant. The question on the table: "What story are we refusing to be defined by? What are we building instead?"
DAY 59
San Félix, Medellín
Air
Group Paragliding + Rock Climbing
Morning
Group paragliding from San Félix — one by one, the Andes valley opens beneath each person. The city below is tiny. Everything that felt enormous is not. This is elevation as perspective, repeated 15 times for 15 different people.
Afternoon
Rock climbing with local Medellín climbers — not expats, locals. Find the community, train with them. The city was built on hillsides; these people have turned the whole city into a vertical training ground.
Evening
Medellín at night. The city is alive. Let the group explore it — the nightlife, the food, the people, the music. This city knows how to celebrate the fact that it is still alive. Be part of that.
DAY 60
Medellín
Urban
Parkour + Urban Movement + Motorbike Explore
Morning
Full day with Medellín's freerunning/parkour community. The city was built on hillsides — movement through it is inherently vertical and creative. These practitioners have turned the whole city into a training ground. One day with them changes how you see every city.

Service Rule
Ask the program coordinator what they actually need — not what feels good to offer. Provide that, even if it's less exciting than what you planned. The distinction is the difference between service and charity tourism.

Day 84
Day One
Arrival + The Opening

New participants arrive. The group welcomes them — not as a program, as people. Cold plunge as the first shared thing. Opening circle, no phones: one sentence each on why they came. The group holds the circle. This is the first time the group has been on this side of it.

Day 85
Day Two

Group designs and leads a challenging physical day — hike, swim, something that pushes new participants to their edge. The group members who struggled in NZ Week 1 are now the ones holding space for others who struggle. They know exactly what to say because they remember how it felt.

Day 86
Day Three
Service Together

Both groups — the 90-day group and the new participants — do service together with a local Costa Rican youth organization. The 90-day group leads. They show the new participants what service without ego looks like. They learned it the hard way. Now they teach it by example.

Day 87
Day Four
Adrenaline + Honesty

Canyoneering, cliff jumping, or whatever the group designed — something that requires full presence. The 90-day group goes first and goes hard. They model what it looks like to move toward fear instead of away from it. The new participants watch. Then they go.

Day 88
Day Five
Solo Day

Everyone — both groups — gets 4 hours alone in nature. No phones. Including the 90-day group. This is their last solo of the program. The evening circle is the most honest one of the week. It always is. The group facilitates it. They know what questions to ask.

Day 89
Day Six
Create + Share

Everyone creates something that reflects who they actually are — a letter, a drawing, movement, anything. Then shares it. The 90-day group goes first. They show what it looks like to be real in front of people. They didn't know how to do this in NZ Week 1. They do now.

Day 90
Day Seven
The Closing Circle

Final cold plunge together. Closing circle. Three questions — everyone answers, both groups: "Who did you arrive as? Who are you leaving as? What are you doing differently?" The 90-day group answers last. They have the most to say. After the last person speaks: one full minute of silence. Then it's done. The world school has its first proof of concept.
