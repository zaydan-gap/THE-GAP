The North Star

The feeling.
You've had it once.

On a summit. In freefall. In the ocean before anyone else is awake. A moment where the past and future disappear and only now exists. Where you are exactly who you actually are — nothing performed, nothing held back.

THE GAP is built around that feeling. Not as entertainment. As the actual curriculum. 90 days of learning what creates it, how to access it deliberately, and how to build a life that keeps returning to it.

"Most people will look at this and say no. That's the point. This is for the ones who feel the gap between who they are and who they're pretending to be — and are ready to do something wild about it."
— THE GAP
Pillar 01
Adventure + Adrenaline

In the three seconds before the jump, every mask falls. The program uses adrenaline as a tool — not for entertainment but for truth. Your body knows things your mind won't admit.

Pillar 02
Travel + Encounter

New Zealand, Bali, Thailand, Colombia, Costa Rica. You travel to encounter — masters, communities, cultures, and landscapes that put everything ordinary in its proper size.

Pillar 03
Service + Giving Back

Show up. Move alongside people. Leave them more capable. Not charity — two groups of humans showing up fully for each other. The program ends with you leading others through it.

Phase 1 · Days 1–30
Decondition
New Zealand

The group arrives as strangers. Cold water, hard terrain, and shared adrenaline turn them into something else. New Zealand strips everything down — who are you when comfort is gone and performance costs too much energy?

  • Bungee bridge · Skydive 15,000ft · Canyon swing
  • Maori forest + whakapapa (2,000-year-old trees)
  • Milford Sound group kayak
  • Overnight in backcountry · Glacier hike
  • Whitewater rafting · Group surf competition
  • Weekly solo time in wild country
Phase 2 · Days 31–57
Encounter
Bali + Thailand

Meet masters who've done it their whole lives. Go underwater on one breath. Train twice a day until thinking becomes impossible. But the learning doesn't only come from the teacher — it comes from the person next to you in the water, the one who gets up on Day 9 when you don't want to. Everyone teaches. Everyone learns. That's what makes it work.

  • AIDA freediving certification (Amed, Bali)
  • 14-day Muay Thai camp (Chiang Mai)
  • Wim Hof breathwork · Active volcano sunrise
  • Uluwatu dawn surf · Kecak fire dance
  • Group fight night · Night dive
Phase 3 · Days 58–90
Return
Colombia + Costa Rica

Everything found gets given back. Serve communities. Stand in the Amazon. Then lead new people through a 7-day version of what the group survived. This is where THE GAP becomes something bigger than one trip.

  • Medellín paragliding + 3-day youth service
  • Amazon indigenous community stay
  • Pacuare whitewater · Arenal volcano
  • Costa Rica canyoneering · Pacific surf
  • 7-day prototype program (the group leads it)

Where It Starts

Micro Immersions.
This is the door.

There's a feeling most people touch once and spend the rest of their lives chasing. Fully present. No gap between where you are and where you're supposed to be. Micro immersions exist to take you back there — deliberately, with people who are actually in it.

"The gap is the space between who you are and who you're capable of becoming. These immersions are built to close it — one practice, one week, one real experience at a time."

— Zaydan Verjee, Founder
Why short?

5–14 days of total immersion bridges more gap than most people close in a year of ordinary life. The timeline is short. The depth isn't.

Why one thing?

Most people sample all their lives and never go deep enough to feel what a discipline actually gives. One practice. One teacher. One group. That's when it lands.

Why with others?

The master teaches the practice. The group teaches you who you are under pressure. Both are essential. Neither works without the other.

The Model

Everyone is both.
Always.

Every person in the room is a student and a teacher at the same time. You come to learn from a master — and you will. But you'll also learn something from the person next to you that the master couldn't have given you. And you'll teach them something back without even knowing it. That's the design. Not a class. A collective.

The Standard

Masters who've
lived it fully.

Every immersion is built around someone who has dedicated their life to what they teach — not a certification, a life. They bring depth you can't find in a course. But the group brings something the master can't always see anymore: fresh eyes, honest questions, and the kind of energy that makes a room come alive. Both are essential. Neither works without the other.

Immersions form around real opportunities — the right teacher, the right place, the right moment. Sign up and we'll reach out when one opens that fits you.

Why I built this

I'm not building this because I figured out some formula. I'm building it because I felt the gap — the distance between who I was performing to be and who I actually was underneath all of it — and went looking for something that could close it.

Outward Bound showed me what was possible when you strip everything down. No phone, no routine, no performance. Just terrain, weather, and people who need each other to make it through. That feeling — of being completely present, completely yourself, completely alive — became the thing I couldn't stop chasing. And at some point, couldn't stop thinking about how to give to other people.

I've been in law offices and I've been in the ocean at dawn and I know which one felt like me. The reason this program exists is that the world doesn't have enough people who feel fully alive. Who move toward things instead of away from them. Who know who they are when the noise stops. THE GAP is how I know how to help with that.

What I believe

Movement is not optional — it's the foundation. Your body is the vehicle for everything else. Take care of it. Push it. Learn what it can do when you stop holding back. Then go live from that place.

Water has something the land doesn't. There's a reason I keep ending up in the ocean, on rivers, under the surface. The ocean resets something. Frees something. I build water into everything I design because it does work that nothing else can.

The best experiences are the ones you share. The summit is different with someone next to you. The hard moment becomes something you survived together. I believe in adventure deeply — and I believe it's better with people you trust.

Teaching is one of the highest forms of service. The best guides don't create dependence — they create capacity. And the thing I keep coming back to: the best learning environments aren't one-directional. Everyone in the room is a student. Everyone in the room is a teacher. The master brings depth and lived experience. The group brings presence, honesty, and the kind of energy that makes something come alive. You need both. That's what I'm trying to build.

What I'm chasing

Zest. The real kind — not the performative version you post about, but the quiet electricity of being exactly where you're supposed to be, doing exactly what you're supposed to be doing, with exactly who you're supposed to be doing it with.

That feeling comes from integrity — from living the life that is actually yours instead of the one you inherited. From adventure, yes. From movement, yes. But most of all from love — for the people you're with, for the experiences you're having, for the world you're moving through.

That's the target. Everything else is just how you get there.

The Full Game

A framework for living fully. Built underneath everything THE GAP does.

Ikigai
Your reason for being

Not a job title. A feeling. Found at the intersection of what you love, what you're genuinely good at, what the world needs from you, and what sustains you. You don't think your way there — you live your way there, by paying attention to when you feel most alive and following that signal relentlessly.

Meraki
Doing things with soul

To pour yourself in completely. To leave a piece of yourself in everything you touch. The opposite of going through the motions. Every pillar in this framework only works if you're bringing meraki to it — because without soul, even the right actions are hollow.

🧬
Temple

Body, mind, stillness. Your vehicle for everything else. Train hard, recover well, find the quiet. Then go.

🌍
Adventure

Life is wide. Find the edges. Nature, water, wild places. Follow the feeling in your chest before it starts — that's the signal.

🤝
Connection

The whole point. Family, friends, love — non-negotiable. Adventure is better shared. The people are the point.

🎯
Service

Bring others along. Great guides create capacity, not dependence. You don't just want it for yourself.

🧠
Growth

Stay a student and a teacher — always both. Seek masters. Share what you know. The best rooms are full of people doing both at once.
