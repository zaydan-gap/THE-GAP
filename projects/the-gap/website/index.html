The North Star

Most people feel it once.

On a summit. In freefall. In the ocean at dawn. The night they drove somewhere they'd never been with people they trusted completely. A feeling of being fully, completely alive — where past and future collapse and only this moment exists. Where you are exactly who you actually are. Nothing performed. Nothing held back.

Then it ends. And they spend the rest of their lives looking for it — in achievement, relationships, the next trip, the next rush. Most people never figure out what made it happen, let alone how to return to it.

THE GAP doesn't just give you the feeling. It shows you what creates it, how to access it deliberately, and how to build a life that keeps returning to it. That's what 90 days is for.

"Most people will look at this and say no. That's the point. This is for the ones who feel the gap between who they are and who they're pretending to be — and are ready to do something wild about it."

— The Gap, 2026

90 days to find out who you are when the noise stops.

School gave you a story about yourself. Society reinforced it. Your feed, your peers, your family — all of it built a version of you that may have nothing to do with who you actually are underneath the noise.

This is not a gap year. It is not a retreat. It is 90 days of the hardest, most alive things the world has to offer — with a group of people chasing the same feeling.

Three Phases

Strip. Encounter. Return.

Phase 1 · Days 1–30
Decondition
New Zealand · South Island + Northland

The group arrives as strangers. Cold water, hard terrain, and shared adrenaline turn them into something else. New Zealand strips everything down to the signal — who are you when you have nothing to perform?

  • Bungee bridge · Skydive 15,000ft · Canyon swing
  • Maori forest + whakapapa (2,000-year-old trees)
  • Milford Sound group kayak
  • Overnight in the wilderness
  • Glacier hike + wild river swimming
  • Group surf competition
Phase 2 · Days 31–57
Encounter
Bali, Indonesia · Chiang Mai, Thailand

Meet masters. Go underwater on one breath in complete silence. Train twice a day until you can't think anymore. The group absorbs what total commitment looks like from the inside — and sees each other without masks for the first time.

  • AIDA freediving certification (Amed)
  • 14-day Muay Thai full camp (Chiang Mai)
  • Wim Hof group breathwork · Volcano sunrise
  • Uluwatu dawn surf · Kecak fire dance
  • Group fight night · Night dive
  • Train with the local kids
Phase 3 · Days 58–90
Return
Colombia · Costa Rica

The group has been stripped and rebuilt. Now they give something back. Serve communities. Stand in the Amazon. Paraglide the Andes. Then lead new people through a 7-day version of what they survived. This is where the world school begins.

  • Medellín paragliding + urban movement
  • Amazon indigenous community stay (Leticia)
  • Youth service — 3 days, Medellín
  • Pacuare whitewater · Volcano hike
  • 7-day prototype program (group leads it)
  • Closing circle — the world school begins

What Every Day Looks Like

Wild every day.
Together the whole way.

🧊
Cold Every Morning

Ocean, river, or cold shower — as a group, before anything else. Competitive, loud, cold. The one practice that turns strangers into a crew within a week.

⚡
One Wild Thing Daily

Every single day has at least one thing most people would never do. Cliff jump, swim a river, climb something, fight someone, go underwater. No filler days.

🌊
Water Mastery

Surf, freedive, kayak, wild swim. The ocean teaches things the land can't. By Day 90 the group's relationship with water is completely different from what it was on Day 1.

🥊
14 Days in a Ring

Muay Thai twice a day. Day 4 you want to quit. That's the point. By Day 10 something shifts. The group trains together, suffers together, shows up for each other.

🌿
Solo Time Weekly

Once a week — hours alone in nature, no phone. The group gives you momentum. The solo gives you truth. Both are required. You'll come back changed every time.

🔥
Campfire Every Night

Group dinner, no phones at the table. The stories that come out in the evening are the ones no one could have told at the beginning. This is where the group becomes real.

🙏
Encounter Masters

Maori elders. Muay Thai champions. Freediving instructors. Amazonian guides. People who chose their lives completely. You absorb what that looks like from the inside.

🤲
Give Something Real

Lead a morning with local youth. Run a service day. End the program leading 10 new people through 7 days. The group that teaches is different from the one that only learns.

The Founder

Zay
Founder, THE GAP · Guide · Builder

I'm not building this because I figured out some formula. I'm building it because I felt the gap — the distance between who I was performing being and who I actually was underneath — and went looking for something that could close it.

Outward Bound showed me what was possible when you strip everything down: no phone, no routine, just terrain and weather and people who need each other to make it through. That feeling — of being completely present, completely yourself, completely alive — became the thing I couldn't stop chasing. And then, at some point, couldn't stop thinking about how to give to other people.

I've been in law offices and I've been in the ocean at dawn and I know which one felt like me. I love water, movement, wild places, and the kind of conversations that only happen after something genuinely hard. I believe in taking care of the body as the foundation for everything else — and then going hard at life with every tool you've built.

The reason this program exists is simple: the world doesn't have enough people who feel alive. Who move toward things instead of away from them. Who know who they are when the noise stops. THE GAP is how I know how to help with that.

The Full Game

A framework for living fully.

Two untranslatable words. Five pillars. One daily principle. This is the philosophy underneath THE GAP — and the thing every participant goes home with.

The Foundation
Ikigai × Meraki

Ikigai — your reason for being. Not a job title. A feeling. Found at the intersection of what you love, what you're genuinely good at, what the world needs from you, and what sustains you. You don't think your way there. You live your way there.

Meraki — to do something with soul. To pour yourself in completely. To leave a piece of yourself in everything you touch. Every pillar only works if you're bringing this to it.

"Life is a game you play
with the ones you love."
— The Full Game
🧬
Temple
Body, mind, stillness. Your vehicle for everything else. Take care of the temple. Then go play.
🌍
Adventure
The wide world. Resfeber. Fernweh. Seek nature, water, wild places. Say yes to the thing that gives you the electric feeling in your chest.
🤝
Connection
The foundation, not a pillar. Family, friends, love — non-negotiable. Adventure is always better shared. The people are the point.
🎯
Service
Bring others along. The best guides don't create dependence — they create capacity. You don't just want it for yourself.
🧠
Growth
Stay a student. Seek great teachers. The moment you think you've figured it out is the moment you stop growing.

Who This Is For

Most people say no.
That's the point.

This is for you if —
  • You feel the gap between who you are and who you're pretending to be
  • You move toward hard things, not away from them
  • You want friendships built on something real — not just circumstance
  • You're 17–24 and know the path you were handed isn't the only one
  • You love water, mountains, movement, and being genuinely pushed
  • You want to give something back in a way that actually means something
  • You're willing to look stupid while learning something real
  • You want to chase the feeling — and learn how to keep returning to it
This is not for you if —
  • You need constant comfort and guaranteed outcomes
  • You're looking for a resume line or Instagram content trip
  • You won't show up for the group when it's inconvenient
  • You think you already have it figured out
  • You want a structured program with a certificate at the end
  • You're not willing to be honest in front of other people
  • You'd rather observe than participate
