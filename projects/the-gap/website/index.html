The North Star

The feeling.
You've had it once.

On a summit. In freefall. In the ocean before anyone else is awake. The past and future disappear. You're exactly who you are — nothing performed, nothing held back.

THE GAP is built around that feeling. Not as entertainment — as the actual curriculum. 90 days of learning what creates it, how to get back to it on purpose, and how to build a life that keeps returning to it.

"Most people will look at this and say no. That's the point. This is for the ones who feel the gap between who they are and who they're pretending to be — and are ready to do something wild about it."
— THE GAP
Pillar 01
Adventure + Adrenaline

In the three seconds before the jump, every mask falls. Adrenaline is a tool here — not for fun, but for truth. Your body knows things your mind won't say out loud.

Pillar 02
Travel + Encounter

New Zealand, Bali, Thailand, Colombia, Costa Rica. You go to encounter masters, communities, cultures, and landscapes that make everything ordinary feel its actual size.

Pillar 03
Service + Giving Back

Show up. Move alongside people. Leave them better. Not charity — two groups showing up fully for each other. The program ends with you leading others through it.

Phase 1 · Days 1–30
Decondition
New Zealand

The group arrives as strangers. Cold water, hard terrain, shared adrenaline. New Zealand strips everything down — who are you when comfort is gone and performing costs too much energy to bother with?

  • Bungee bridge · Skydive 15,000ft · Canyon swing
  • Waipoua Forest · ancient kauri trees (2,000+ years old)
  • Milford Sound group kayak
  • Overnight in backcountry · Glacier hike
  • Whitewater rafting · Group surf competition
  • Weekly solo time in wild country
Phase 2 · Days 31–57
Encounter
Bali + Thailand

Meet masters who've done it their whole lives. Go underwater on one breath. Train twice a day until thinking stops being possible. The learning doesn't only come from the teacher — it comes from the person next to you in the water, the one who shows up to training on the morning you've already decided to quit. Everyone teaches. Everyone learns.

  • AIDA freediving certification (Amed, Bali)
  • 14-day Muay Thai camp (Chiang Mai)
  • Wim Hof breathwork · Active volcano sunrise
  • Uluwatu dawn surf · Kecak fire dance
  • Group fight night · Night dive
Phase 3 · Days 58–90
Return
Colombia + Costa Rica

Everything found gets given back. Serve communities. Stand in the Amazon. Then the group leads a Micro Immersion — taking a new set of people through what they just learned. That's when it stops being a trip and becomes something bigger.

  • Medellín paragliding + 3-day youth service
  • Amazon indigenous community stay
  • Pacuare whitewater · Arenal volcano
  • Costa Rica canyoneering · Pacific surf
  • 7-day prototype program (the group leads it)

Where It Starts

Micro Immersions.
This is the door.

There's a feeling most people touch once and spend the rest of their lives chasing. Fully present. No gap between where you are and where you're supposed to be. Micro immersions are built to take you back there — on purpose, with people who are all in.

This is where it actually gets fun. You go deep on something most people only ever watch from the outside — freediving, Muay Thai, mountaineering, surfing — and you get genuinely good at it. Not dabble-and-move-on good. Real good. The kind that changes how you handle pressure, how you trust yourself, how you move through everything else.

Every immersion runs the same framework: Adventure + Adrenaline — hard, physical, real. Travel + Encounter — you go somewhere that puts you in the presence of mastery. Service + Giving Back — you don't just take the experience. You add to it. That's what makes it land.

"The gap is the space between who you are and who you're capable of becoming. These immersions are built to close it — one practice, one week, one real experience at a time."

— Zaydan Verjee, Founder
Why short?

5–14 days of total immersion closes more gap than most people close in a year of regular life. The timeline is short. The depth isn't.

Why one thing?

Most people sample all their lives and never go deep enough to feel what a discipline really gives. One practice. One teacher. One group. That's when it lands.

Why with others?

The master teaches the practice. The group teaches you who you are under pressure. You need both.

The Model

Everyone is both.
Always.

Everyone in the room is a student and a teacher at the same time. You come to learn from the master — and you will. But you'll also learn something from the person next to you that the master couldn't give you. And you'll teach them something back without knowing it. That's the design. Not a class. A collective.

The Standard

Masters who've
lived it fully.

Every immersion is built around someone who has dedicated their life to what they teach — not a certification, a life. They bring depth you can't find in a course. The group brings what the master can't always see anymore: fresh eyes, honest questions, and the kind of energy that makes a room actually alive. You need both.

Immersions form around real opportunities — the right teacher, the right place, the right moment. Sign up and we'll reach out when one opens that fits you.

The Founder
Seljalandsfoss, Iceland
Zaydan Verjee
Founder, THE GAP · Guide · Adventurer · Builder
Why I built this

I'm not building this because I figured out some formula. I'm building it because I felt the gap — the distance between who I was pretending to be and who I actually was — and went looking for something that could close it.

Outward Bound showed me what's possible when you strip everything down. No phone, no routine, no performance. Just terrain, weather, and people who need each other to get through it. That feeling — completely present, completely yourself, completely alive — became the thing I couldn't stop chasing. And eventually couldn't stop thinking about how to give to other people.

I've been in law offices and I've been in the ocean at dawn and I know which one felt like me. The world doesn't have enough people who feel fully alive. Who move toward things instead of away from them. Who know who they are when the noise stops. THE GAP is how I know how to help with that.

What I believe

Movement isn't optional — it's the foundation. Your body is the vehicle for everything else. Take care of it. Push it. Find out what it can do when you stop holding back. Then live from that place.

Water has something the land doesn't. There's a reason I keep ending up in the ocean, on rivers, under the surface. It resets something. Frees something. I build water into everything I design because it does work nothing else can.

The best experiences are the ones you share. The summit is different with someone next to you. The hard moment becomes something you survived together. Adventure is better with people you actually trust.

Teaching is one of the highest forms of service. The best guides create capacity, not dependence. The best learning rooms aren't one-directional — everyone's a student, everyone's a teacher. The master brings depth. The group brings presence and honesty. You need both. That's what I'm trying to build.

What I'm chasing

Zest. The real kind — not the version you post about, but the quiet electricity of being exactly where you're supposed to be, with exactly who you're supposed to be with, doing exactly what you're supposed to be doing.

That comes from integrity. From living the life that's actually yours instead of the one you inherited. From adventure, yes. From movement, yes. But mostly from love — for the people you're with, for the world you're moving through.

That's the target. Everything else is just how you get there.

The Full Game

A framework for living fully — underneath everything THE GAP does.

Ikigai
Your reason for being

Not a job title. A feeling. The intersection of what you love, what you're genuinely good at, what the world needs, and what sustains you. You don't think your way there — you live your way there. Pay attention to when you feel most alive and follow that relentlessly.

Meraki
Doing things with soul

To pour yourself in completely. Leave a piece of yourself in everything you touch. The opposite of going through the motions. Every pillar in this framework only works if you're bringing meraki to it — without soul, even the right actions are hollow.

🧬
Temple

Body, mind, stillness. Your vehicle for everything else. Train hard, recover well, find the quiet. Then go.

🌍
Adventure

Life is wide. Find the edges. Nature, water, wild places. Follow the feeling in your chest before it starts — that's the signal.

🤝
Connection

The whole point. Family, friends, love — non-negotiable. Adventure is better shared. The people are the point.

🎯
Service

Bring others along. Great guides create capacity, not dependence. You don't just want it for yourself.

🧠
Growth

Stay a student and a teacher — always both. Seek masters. Share what you know. The best rooms are full of people doing both at once.
